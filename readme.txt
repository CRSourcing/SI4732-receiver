
Firmware for SI473x receivers with ESP32 and ILI9341 display driver. Works on hardware based on the "common" schematic that has been published several times. An example schematic 
can be found in the pics folder. The audio feed to GPIO32 can be done via a LM386, or a singe transistor amplifier with 1.65V at the collector.

Hints:

Start:

1. Upload .bin file to ESP32.
2.Config display and touchscreen. The encoder pushbutton pin must use a resistive divider to work (see schematic). If the encoder is reversed, change encoder direction in the Config menu.
3. Tap More -> Config -> WiFi Cred and configure your WiFi credentials. Receiver will work without WiFi, but several functions will not work.



Hardware:

Audio feed into ADC:
An audio feed into GPIO32 is required for waterfall, decoders and audio dsp:
Requires an 1-2Vpp audio signal on GPIO32, centered at 1.65V. This can be provided via a LM386 (ATS25 max decoder or max decoder conversion), or a single
transistor amplifier accordingly biased. A 5 - 10nF cap from GPIO32 to ground is strongly recommended to reduce undersampling / aliasing artefacts.



Audio feedback into audio amplifier:
Audio DSP, touch confirmation sound and SD card "Play audio": These requires an additional 10nF/15K combination in series from GPIO26 to the input of the audio amplifier.
The resistor determines volume. This will feed audio generated by the DAC to the audio channel(s).

Audio DSP requires the audio signal from SI4732 to be blocked from the audio amps when active. Otherwise you will hear the original audio together with the digital audio. The audio DSP receives the signal via GPIO32, processes it and sends it to the audio amp(s) through the  10nF/15K combination(s). This is NOT ATS25 compatible. Some receivers use a squelch transistor. This will work, if you feed the digital audio back after the squelch transistor, for example directly to the input of the audio amp. Some experiments may be needed. 


SD card: Gets connected to the SPI bus (in parallel to the display), plus SD_CS, which gets connected to GPIO25. Short lines are required (20MHz on the bus).
The common ILI9341 display has an integrated SD card slot. Not all SD cards work. If card is not recognized, turn receiver completely off and try another.
Connect:
SCK  -> GPIO18
MISO -> GPIO19   MISO=SDO
MOSI -> GPIO23   MOSI=SDI
SD_CS ->GPIO25

Encoder:
Press the encoder for fine tune mode. Press again to leave.


Battery Voltage:
Needs to be read trough a 2/1 resistive divider on GPIO33. Do not exceed 6.6V battery voltage.



Software:

WiFi Sync (in Storage Menu). Use to download station lists from receiver, edit them and re-upload. Also for downloading SSTV image files.


WiFi interface: Set volume correctly: First adjust the receivers volume to be loud enough, but stay below distortion and clipping.
Then use the WiFi interface volume slider to adjust volume.
If range is insufficient, try do change the adc divier rate (debug menu). The WiFi interface requires full processing power of the ESP32, so other functions
may not be available, or respond delayed. To leave WiFi interface, press the Freq button and restart with the "Boot" button.


Eibi viewer:
Lists all stations on given freq. Transmitting stations will be shown in green, all others in white.


CW Decoder:
Works best between 500 and 800Hz. Align signal with waterfall red bars. Move encoder slowly to align. If may "t" or "e"'s are displayed, decoder isn't synchroizing, change volume.
Press encoder to leave.


RTTY Decoder:
Does not automatically load modulation or bandwidth. For FSK, offsets must be added to FREQ. Use the waterfall to align mark and space with the bars.
Load decoder and use encoder to fine tune if needed. Red/green bars should have similar heights.

SSTV Decoder:
Will decode Martin and Scottie modes. Autodetect tries to detect the mode based on the norizontal sync interval (not VIS), but may fail if signal is weak.
The image can be saved either on the internal LittleFS file system, or an SD card. Saving on SD card will always be done in .bmp format. Saving on LittleFS can be done
in .raw format (1 byte per pixel, 255 colors, 332bits , no header), or .bmp format. Around 12 .raw or 4 .bmp files can be saved on LittleFS.
Files have consecutive numbering and the oldest file will be overwritten if a new file gets saved and no space is available. A file viewer in the storage menu is available.
Files can also be downloaded via "WiFi sync".


Weatherfax - Under development. Currently only IOC567 format supported.

Indicators:
Press indicators (below S Meter scale) to change their values. Press left or right of the "Step:" indicator on the right to move frequency one step up or down.

Frequency digits:
Press frequency digits (upper or lower half) to change their value. This requires precise touch calibration. Will not work with "nixie" digits.
